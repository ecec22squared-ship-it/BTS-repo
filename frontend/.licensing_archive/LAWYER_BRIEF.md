# 30-Minute Lawyer Brief

Give this to the games-industry IP attorney before your consultation so they can hit the ground running. Budget $400–$800 for their one-hour redline. It is the single highest-ROI expense in this entire process.

## 1. Who we are
- **Name:** EC² Gaming (indie, solo/small team, no prior shipped titles)
- **Contact:** universal4050@gmail.com
- **App:** Beyond the Stars (mobile text RPG, Expo + FastAPI)
- **Status:** Internal technical demo complete, **not publicly released, no app-store submission**

## 2. What we want
- Send **one** polite written inquiry to Disney Legal Permissions asking whether they will consider a licensing proposal for use of Disney/Lucasfilm IP (specifically, the Star Wars mark and selected characters/species).
- We are **not** publishing the app with those marks unless and until a signed license exists.
- We expect the inquiry to be ignored or declined; we are doing it in good faith so we can pivot cleanly if rejected.

## 3. What we need from you, counselor
1. Review and redline the attached `PITCH_LETTER.md` and `PITCH_DECK.html` for any language that:
   - accidentally asserts affiliation, endorsement, or license
   - commits us to commercial terms
   - waives our rights or creates admissions about prior use
2. Confirm the inquiry channel (Disney Legal Permissions form) is appropriate, or recommend a better route.
3. Advise whether the pitch should go under personal name vs. a filed LLC, and whether to spin up EC² Gaming LLC first.
4. Tell us what to include/exclude about the existing technical demo — specifically whether screenshots or gameplay video should be attached or withheld until an NDA is in place.
5. Draft a one-paragraph NDA-acceptance clause we can include, in case Disney requests more material.

## 4. Existing exposure we want your read on
- The internal demo currently uses Star Wars-owned trademarks in its text (species, factions, era names). It has **never been released to the public**, but it is visible on a development preview URL behind Google sign-in.
- There is an in-app magenta disclaimer: "This Game is not Endorsed, Supported, or Affiliated with Star Wars or any associated company."
- Your job: tell us whether the preview URL itself creates meaningful exposure, and if so, what to lock down today.

## 5. The fork we need your opinion on
- **Path A (pitch first):** send the redlined inquiry, wait ~30 days. If no green light, rebrand all in-app trademark references to original names and publish to the stores.
- **Path B (rebrand first):** rebrand now, publish rebranded version, then — only after gaining traction — approach Disney *through a licensing agent* about a Star Wars skin / DLC.
- Which is less legally risky and more likely to succeed commercially?

## 6. Our budget & timeline
- Your review: this week.
- Inquiry submission: within 14 days of your sign-off.
- Rebrand-and-ship decision: within 45 days regardless of Disney's response.

## 7. Our hard commitments
- We will not publish the app publicly with Disney/Lucasfilm marks without a signed license.
- We will not contact Disney by any channel other than the one you approve.
- We will not represent to the public, press, or social media that a licensing conversation is underway.
