## ✅ What this letter deliberately does NOT do (and why)

| NOT doing | Why |
| --- | --- |
| Use the words "Star Wars", "Jedi", "Sith", or any character name | Zero unauthorized trademark use in the letter itself → zero infringement exposure |
| Offer any revenue split, royalty, or MG figure | Terms are the *licensor's* template. Your offer boxes you in. |
| Attach gameplay footage or in-app screenshots that show the IP | Shared evidence of infringement. Don't. Send it later *if* they ask and an NDA is in place. |
| CC any executive, press outlet, or third party | Scatter-shot outreach is bad-faith by definition |
| Claim existing or prior endorsement, affiliation, or "40% offered" | False claims / unilateral offers are never binding and can create fraud exposure |
| Mention the in-app magenta disclaimer | Irrelevant to licensing, complicates the narrative |

## ✍️ What your lawyer should help you decide before sending

1. Should this go under your personal name or a filed EC² Gaming LLC? (Usually: LLC if it exists; else personal, with LLC formation noted as in-progress.)
2. Do you want to include a U.S. mailing address (often expected) — use a virtual business address service if you don't have a formal office.
3. Has EC² Gaming registered its trademark? Mentioning a pending EC² mark signals seriousness.
4. NDA template ready? Disney will only look at creative work under their NDA, not yours.
5. Is the **PITCH_DECK.html → PDF** free of Disney trademarks, character names, and visual IP? (It should be; verify with your attorney.)
