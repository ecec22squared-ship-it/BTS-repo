# Disney / Lucasfilm Licensing Contacts

*Last verified: April 2026 from Disney's own public pages (thewaltdisneycompany.com, disneylicensing.com, disneystudiolicensing.com, lucasfilm.com).*

Only publicly listed, non-private contact points are shown. **Do NOT** try to find personal executive email addresses — doing so destroys your credibility.

---

## 1. The only legitimate front doors

### 🥇 Disney Legal Permissions & Image Licensing (highest-probability first step)
- **URL:** https://thewaltdisneycompany.com/contact-us/
- **Channel:** "Legal Permissions and Image Licensing" form on the contact-us page
- **Who it reaches:** Disney Corporate Legal intake team
- **Use for:** Written *inquiry* about licensing a trademarked IP (this is what you file first)
- **What to attach:** `PITCH_LETTER.md` content + `PITCH_DECK.html` exported to PDF
- **Realistic reply window:** 0–8 weeks. Most commonly: no reply.
- **Probability of a substantive response for an indie with no shipped titles:** ~1–5%.

### 🥈 Disney Consumer Products – Merchandise Licensing (games historically routed here)
- **URL:** https://www.disneylicensing.com
- **Application:** Facility and Merchandise Authorization (FAMA) — PDF on thewaltdisneycompany.com
- **Bar for entry:** Documented 5+ years of relevant manufacturing/distribution. **You won't pass this.** Noted here for completeness only.

### 🥉 Walt Disney Studios Licensing (film/entertainment properties)
- **URL:** https://www.disneystudiolicensing.com
- **Use for:** Clips, stills, music licensing from Disney films — *not* trademark/character licensing for games.
- **Relevance for you:** None, but included so you don't waste a day on the wrong form.

### 🏵️ Lucasfilm Ltd. (San Francisco, CA)
- **Public address:** 1 Letterman Drive, Building B, San Francisco, CA 94129 USA
- **Email for media/press inquiries only:** (listed on lucasfilm.com, rotates — check current page)
- **Public stance:** Does **not** publish a licensing inbox. All commercial inquiries are routed to Disney Corporate (Channel #1 above).
- **Lucasfilm Games** is a brand-stewardship team, *not* a submissions inbox. They are consumers of partner pitches that go through Disney Consumer Products / Interactive channels, never cold email.

## 2. Channels that feel legit but are not

| Looks like... | Reality |
| --- | --- |
| "Disney Executive Office" or "Bob Iger's office" | Assistants delete unsolicited mail. Sending there flags you. |
| LinkedIn messaging a Disney Brand Director | Counts as unsolicited; they block to prevent inbound spam. |
| Press release blasts | Not a pitch channel. |
| Filing an app-store submission hoping Disney will notice | This is **infringement**. Skip. |

## 3. Channels that actually work for indies (the real paths)

These are how indie teams have historically gotten Lucasfilm deals:

| Path | How it works | Cost / effort |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hire a games IP licensing agent** | Boutique agents have pre-existing Lucasfilm/Disney relationships; they pitch on your behalf. Standard fee is 10–25% of any deal. | $5–20K retainer + commission |
| **Get invited at a games licensing event** | Licensing Expo (Las Vegas, spring), GDC (San Francisco, spring), Gamescom (Cologne, summer). Badges ~ $500–1,500. | Travel + badge + time |
| **Partner with an established games publisher** | Aspyr, Nightdive, Dotemu etc. have Disney relationships. You license the IP *through* them. | Royalty share + no direct negotiation |
| **Show traction first, approach second** | Ship the rebranded app, reach 50K+ downloads and 4★+, *then* come back and pitch the Star Wars version as a skin/DLC of proven tech. | 6–12 months |

**Most realistic for you as a solo/small-team indie:** start with the Disney Legal Permissions form (it's free and safe), then pursue "ship rebranded + build traction" in parallel. If and when you have real numbers, an agent will take your call.
