# Licensing Outreach Kit — Disney / Lucasfilm

**Publisher:** EC² Gaming
**App:** Beyond the Stars (v1.0)
**Intent:** Seek formal written authorization from Disney / Lucasfilm to publish the app using their Star Wars intellectual property.

---

## ⚠️ Read This First — Reality Check

I want you to know exactly what you're walking into so nothing surprises you later. I've pulled this from Disney's own publicly posted policies and current industry reporting (sourced 2026).

### The hard facts
1. **Disney's written corporate policy explicitly states** they do not accept unsolicited creative submissions or licensing proposals.
2. **Lucasfilm Games** (the division that would handle Star Wars video games) **does not publish a licensing contact**. Their model is to pick one studio per franchise via direct invitation (EA, Respawn, Aspyr, Quantic Dream, Ubisoft Massive).
3. **Disney Consumer Products** (merchandise licensing) requires **5+ years of manufacturing/distribution history** before they'll even read your FAMA application.
4. Realistic response rate for a cold, un-agented pitch from an unknown indie: **essentially zero**.

### Why we're doing it anyway (it's still worth trying)
- A polite written inquiry that uses *no* trademarks and makes *no* unauthorized public use creates **zero infringement liability**. It's safe to send.
- It creates a paper trail showing good-faith effort.
- On the off chance you land in front of a Brand Director, the pitch package has to be professional — this kit delivers that.
- If the answer is "no" (very likely) or silence (most likely), you move to the **rebrand path** with a clean conscience.

### What you should NOT do
- 🚫 **Do not publish the app to any store while marked with Star Wars IP without a signed license agreement.** Publishing first and offering royalties after is infringement, not licensing.
- 🚫 **Do not offer specific revenue terms (e.g. “40%”) in your pitch.** Licensing terms are *their* template, not yours. Offering terms up-front screams amateur and gives them leverage.
- 🚫 **Do not CC "Executive Office" or mass-mail.** It will be filed as spam and can itself be used as evidence of bad-faith outreach.
- 🚫 **Do not send the pitch email without a games-industry IP attorney reviewing it first.** Even a polite email can contain phrases that complicate later negotiations. Budget **$400–$800** for a one-hour review.
